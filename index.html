From AR Face Tracker to AR Sports
March, 2022
Dec., 2022
Feb., 2024
AR sports is the project I put the most effort into during high school. I started with AR Face Tracker when I was learning game development in early 2022, and continued to improve my skills while optimizing the game in next two years until it was successfully released on the AppStore and Google Play platforms in 2024.
AR Face Tracker
In early 2022, I participated in the Apple WWDC Swift Student Challenge with AR Face Tracker, which uses AR Face Detection technology to track the player's head movement. Players tilted their heads left and right to control the goalkeeper defensing for incoming balls.
This innovative human-computer interaction method helped me win the award.

I had no art skills then, so I could only make ugly scenes like this. The game's creative control methods mainly win the award.
Self-improvement
To port this game to more platforms, I chose Unity, a popular game development tool. In parallel with completing five Unity courses on Coursera, I also learned some knowledge of Photoshop and Blender, which allowed me to improve the game's graphics. At the end of 2022, I remade the game with Unity and applied to CMU's High School Game Design Academy.

I made models of the player and purchased the scene from the Unity Asset Store.
AR Sports
From 2023 to 2024, I continued improving and developing the game. I introduced five more different control methods and created five new mini-games. Finally, I made a sports mini-game collection called AR Sports. In early 2024, I successfully released the game on the App Store and Google Play.
Hellevator! A whimsy game about sorting ghosts into the correct levels of the inferno.
Play as Dante, a twelve-year-old who fell into hell by sheer accident after cheating on his math test!Work for your new "boss", Mephistopheles, as an elevator operator - judging the crimes on the life receipts of ghosts, spirits, souls (whatever you call them) and transporting them to their "eternal punishment"!Gain or lose your boss's trust, all while under the pressure of time (as he has high expectations of your efficiency), or be doomed!
This game was developed during the National High School Game Academy in CMU.
Our team named Peculiar Geese with five members: Megan (production, sound design), Ellie (art), Ashley (art), Alex (programming) and me (programming).
It received the Best Use of TA Resources award for involving all the TAs in voiceover.
Source code and introduced video are available on Github and Youtube now.
